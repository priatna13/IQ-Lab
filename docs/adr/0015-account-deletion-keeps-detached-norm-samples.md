# Account Deletion removes identifiable data; detached Norm Samples remain

Privacy requires Participants can leave with their Results gone, while empirical norms need stable aggregate history. Account Deletion deletes or anonymizes all identifiable Participant data (Attempts, Responses, Result Snapshots, Report artifacts, credentials). Norm Samples already detached from identity (no Account/PII link) remain for norm science and are not “owned” by the deleted Participant. Implementation may cascade-delete after extract or anonymize-in-place; domain outcome is identical.

**Consequences:** Norm Sample extraction must strip identifiers before or during deletion; privacy copy must state aggregates may remain; published Norm Versions are not rewritten person-by-person on delete.
